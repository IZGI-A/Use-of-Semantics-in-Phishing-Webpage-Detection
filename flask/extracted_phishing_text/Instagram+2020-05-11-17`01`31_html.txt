To be original enough for copyright protection, a work needs to be created by the author themselves, and must have some minimal amount of creativity. Generally, things like names, titles, slogans or short phrases aren't considered to be original enough for copyright protection. For example, the symbol “+” is likely not subject to copyright, but a painting full of shapes and colors arranged in a unique pattern is likely protected under copyright.
